# Idea Validation Report

**Idea:** AI tutoring marketplace

**Viability Score: 7/10**

Market=5/5, Competition=2/5, Risk=2/5, Differentiation=5/5 → raw=14 → score=7/10

## Competitors

- **Coursera - Online Courses** (https://coursera.org): Online learning marketplace with AI recommendations
- **Wyzant - Tutor Marketplace** (https://wyzant.com): Connect with expert tutors for personalised learning
- **Chegg Tutors** (https://chegg.com): On-demand tutoring and homework help
- **Preply - Language Tutoring** (https://preply.com): AI-matched tutors for language learning
- **Superprof - Tutor Network** (https://superprof.com): Largest network of tutors worldwide

## Market Estimate

- TAM: $50,000,000,000
- SAM: $5,000,000,000
- SOM: $100,000,000

The global tutoring and supplemental education market is large and growing, driven by demand
for personalised learning and rising adoption of AI. TAM includes all online education spend;
SAM focuses on marketplace-style tutor platforms; SOM reflects a realistic 2% capture in the
first 3 years.

## Top Risks

- [HIGH] **Regulatory compliance**: EdTech regulations vary widely by jurisdiction;
  data-privacy rules (COPPA, GDPR) add compliance burden for student data.
- [MEDIUM] **Tutor supply quality**: Onboarding and vetting quality tutors at scale is
  operationally hard; low quality erodes trust quickly.
- [LOW] **Student churn after goal completion**: Students may cancel once they achieve their
  learning goal, limiting lifetime value.

## Key Differentiators

- **AI-powered tutor matching**: Proprietary matching algorithm reduces search friction and
  improves first-session satisfaction versus manual filtering.
- **Verified tutor credentials**: Background checks and credential verification build parent
  and student trust, differentiating from peer-to-peer platforms.
- **Progress tracking dashboard**: Data-driven learning plans with session analytics help
  students stay on track and give parents visibility.
